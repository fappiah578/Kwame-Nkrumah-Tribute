Kwame Nkrumah

The First President of the Republic of Ghana

Kwame Nkrumah (21 September 1909 - 27 April 1972) was a Ghanaian politician, political theorist, and revolutionary. He was the first Prime Minister and President of Ghana, having led the Gold Coast to independence from Britain in 1957. An influential advocate of Pan-Africanism, Nkrumah was a founding member of the Organization of African Unity and winner of the Lenin Peace Prize from the Soviet Union in 1962.

Here is timeline of his life:

  • 1909- Kwame Nkrumah was born 21 September, 1909 in Nkroful.
  • 1930 - He graduated from Achimota College and started his career as a teacher for Roman Catholic junior schools for Elmina and Axim.
  • 1935 - He gained admission to the Lincoln University in United States to further his education.
  • 1947 - He took up a position in United Gold Coast Convention (UGCC) in 1947 upon invitation from the party leadership.
  • 1949 - Nkrumah announced the formation of Convention People's Party
  • 1951 - But while in jail, Gold Coast held the first general election on February 8, 1951 where the CPP candidate, Kwame Nkrumah won the elections.
  • 1972 - He died of prostate cancer in April 1972 at the age of 62 while in Romania.
